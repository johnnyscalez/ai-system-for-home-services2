The Night Shift Your Office Never Had

An after-hours AI employee for HVAC companies that run on Housecall Pro. It answers every lead when the office is closed, books the right tech into the CRM they already use, and proves its revenue every morning. We stopped selling a new CRM. We sell the missing employee.

  • 10:47 PM Homeowner's AC dies. She calls Berg's Heating & Air. Office closed — the AI answers on the second ring: "This is the after-hours assistant for Berg's — what's going on tonight?"
  • 10:49 PM Qualified in four questions: no cooling, running but warm, in service area, homeowner. Zip + job type check pass.
  • 10:51 PM Dispatch match: Mike covers 85251, does residential AC, has an 8:00 AM slot open in Housecall Pro. Job booked — with the full conversation summary in the job notes.
  • 10:52 PM Upsell, per the owner's playbook: "Since we're coming out anyway — our Comfort Club waives the dispatch fee and gets you priority." Sold. No prices quoted, ever.
  • 10:53 PM Mike gets a text: tomorrow 8:00 AM, address, what the system is doing, what was promised. No morning surprises.
  • 2:14 AM Second caller smells gas. Hard-coded interrupt — "hang up and call 911 and your gas company right now" — owner notified. Not AI judgment. A fire alarm.
  • 7:30 AM Owner's digest: 6 conversations, 3 jobs booked, 1 membership sold, 2 callbacks needed by 8:15. The calendar filled itself while he slept.

Why we pivoted

Legitimate shops won't switch CRMs. They'll hire for the gap.

Our original product bundled AI follow-up with our own CRM. The market told us something better: the bigger, healthier HVAC companies — our real ICP — already run their entire operation on Housecall Pro. Office staff trained on it. Billing in it. Techs dispatched from it. Asking them to switch is asking them to risk the business to try us. They will always say no.

"I don't need another CRM. I need to wake up with three new jobs on my calendar that got booked while nobody was in the office." — HVAC owner, pilot conversation, July 2026

So the product inverts: their CRM stays the system of record. We install the one employee it's missing — the night shift — and wire everything we do back into their existing workflow. Zero switching cost. Zero retraining. One clear job.

What it is

One brain, four channels, one job: fill the morning calendar

Every after-hours lead — inbound call, SMS, Facebook Messenger, WhatsApp — reaches the same conversational brain. Voice and SMS lead the launch (that's where emergency money lives); Meta channels join when app review clears. If a call is missed or abandoned, the same conversation continues instantly over text — one thread, one memory, no dead ends.

Homeowner

Gets answered at 11 PM by an assistant that says what it is, asks four smart questions, books a real slot, and never quotes a price. Panic → confirmed appointment in under five minutes.

Owner

Wakes to the 7:30 digest and a dashboard with two honest numbers: revenue booked by the AI and revenue sourced by it. The subscription justifies itself every renewal.

Office

Opens Housecall Pro at 8 AM — nothing new to learn. Every AI booking carries the full story in the job notes. Unresolved conversations arrive as a callback list with context, promised to customers "by 8:15."

Tech

Gets a text the moment an overnight emergency lands on his board: time, address, what the customer said. Right tech, right zip, right skill — matched against his live HCP schedule.

Conversation brain

Qualify · handle · book

The existing AI engine and HVAC playbooks, extended with the after-hours activation window and hard guardrails: qualify zip + job type early; never quote prices; owner-configured upsells per service-call type; safety keywords trigger a scripted 911 interrupt; always discloses it's an AI; no 2 AM transfers — summarize, promise the 8:15 callback, notify the owner.

Dispatch engine

The right tech, not just a slot

Per-tech profiles (zip coverage, job types, on-call default — already modeled in our schema) crossed with live availability read from Housecall Pro. Emergencies book tonight's on-call; non-urgent books tomorrow's windows.

↓

Housecall Pro sync

Their system of record

Creates the customer, books the job with conversation summary in the notes, assigns the tech. Reads employees and schedules. We never duplicate what HCP owns — calendar, invoicing, tech portal, reporting stay theirs.

↓

Attribution engine

Prove the money

Every HCP webhook is matched at the customer level: jobs the AI created count as booked by AI; any later job on an AI-sourced customer — the office re-booking, the $8K estimate-to-install conversion — counts as sourced by AI inside a 90-day window. Office edits get tagged, never lost.

↓

Surfaces

Thin by design

The 7:30 AM owner digest (SMS + email), instant tech notifications, and a performance dashboard — conversations, bookings, membership attach rate, attributed revenue. Our full CRM survives as a fallback mode for shops without a supported platform.

Deliberately narrow wedge

"After-hours only" is easy to say yes to — it threatens nobody's job and competes with nothing they've bought. Daytime overflow is the natural expansion upsell.

The attribution moat

Platform AI answers calls. Ours proves dollars: "the AI made you $23,400 this month" is a retention engine no message-taking product can copy without our webhook + customer-level model.

HVAC-deep playbooks

Qualification order, objection handling, membership attach, safety interrupts — earned from real owner feedback, not a generic receptionist script.

Multi-CRM escape hatch

The architecture isolates HCP behind one sync layer. Jobber and ServiceTitan adapters are the countermove if HCP turns competitor — and cold-campaign replies are already telling us which comes second.

Execution

Build phases — validation before construction

  • Kill the risk

    gate: pilot API key

    Owner's HCP API key in hand → validator proves auth, reads his techs and schedule, creates one test job in his live account. If this fails, everything else is theory. Groundwork already shipped: client, webhook receiver, validator script, attribution schema.

  • The core loop

    call → booked job

    Inbound voice answering + after-hours window + missed-call text-back + dispatch matching. One real after-hours call becomes one correctly-assigned HCP job with notes and tech notification.

  • The trust layer

    first bad night prevented

    Safety interrupts, escalation paths, early zip/job-type disqualification, upsell playbook config, AI disclosure. Tested adversarially before the first real customer night.

  • The proof layer

    owner sees money

    Attribution engine live on his webhooks, 7:30 digest, performance dashboard. This is the phase that converts pilot → paying → case study.

  • Widen the funnel

    meta review clears

    Messenger + WhatsApp adapters (24-hour-window-compliant follow-ups), onboarding flow for customer #2, daytime overflow upsell, second-CRM adapter chosen from campaign reply data.

Eyes open

Risks we're carrying deliberately

n = 1 validation

The whole spec leans on one owner's word. Mitigation: the API key request doubles as the commitment test — and phase gates keep spend proportional to evidence. Get 3–5 more HCP-shop conversations before phase 4.

Housecall Pro builds this natively

They have the distribution and the motive. Mitigation: move faster in a niche they serve generically, own the attribution proof, and keep the sync layer swappable so Jobber/ServiceTitan adapters turn platform risk into TAM.

Voice quality is the product

One stumbling 11 PM call as "Berg's Heating & Air" burns trust faster than ten good ones build it. Mitigation: missed-call text-back as the safety net, pilot iteration on real call recordings, and the office-open handoff for anything the AI shouldn't touch.

Meta timelines we don't control

App review takes 2–6 weeks and can bounce. Mitigation: verification already in review; launch is gated on voice + SMS only; Messenger/WhatsApp attach when cleared.
