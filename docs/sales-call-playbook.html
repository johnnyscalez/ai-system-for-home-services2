Call Structure — Phases 1–3
The Call Flow
25–35 Minute Framework
1
Open + Rapport
2–3 minutes
Make them comfortable

These leads already said yes. Don't over-pitch the opener. Be diagnostic, not salesy — this immediately separates you from every vendor they've ever spoken to.

"Hey [Name], thanks for jumping on — I'm [your name]. I saw you're running ads in [city], which is actually why I reached out. Before I show you anything, I just want to make sure this actually makes sense for your situation. Cool if I ask you a couple quick questions first?"

Quick rapport question (pick one):

  • "How long have you been running the [company name] show?"
  • "I saw your Google reviews — a lot of customers mention [thing]. Is that intentional?"
  • "You're in [city] — peak season must be intense right now, how's volume been?"
"Awesome. So let me understand your situation a bit — then I'll show you exactly what I built and you can tell me if it makes sense."
2
Diagnose Pain
8–10 minutes
Get them to say the number

This is the most important phase. Don't tell them their problem — let them tell you. By the time they've answered all 5 questions, they've convinced themselves. Your solution becomes the obvious relief.

Q1
Establish the bleed
"When someone fills out your Facebook form right now — what actually happens? Like, who follows up and how fast?"
If they say "pretty fast" → "What about at 10pm on a Wednesday? Or Saturday morning?"
Q2
Quantify the loss — THE CRITICAL MOMENT
"How many leads do you think slip through — ones where you called but they went cold before you could connect?"
Then: "What's your average job worth — estimate ticket?" → "So [X] leads × $[ticket] = $[X] walking out every month. Does that feel about right?"
Q3
Find the bottleneck
"Are you the one doing most of the follow-up, or do you have someone on it?"
If themselves: "What happens when you're on a job and someone fills out the form?"
Q4
Surface the real fear
"Has this ever cost you a big job? A lead that became someone else's customer because you couldn't get to them in time?"
Q5
Confirm the trigger
"Why did you respond to my message? Is this a timing thing or has it been a problem for a while?"
Always do the pain summary before moving to the demo:
"Okay so let me make sure I'm hearing this right. You're spending [X]/month on ads, generating [X] leads, but a big chunk go cold — especially after hours. And at [ticket size], that's real money. Fair summary?" → When they say yes — PAUSE. Then: "Okay. Let me show you what I built."
3
Show the System
7–8 minutes
Make the outcome vivid

Frame before sharing screen: "I'm going to walk you through exactly what happens from the moment someone fills out your form. Specific to HVAC. Takes 7 minutes."

StepWhat You SayWhat You Show
1. 47-Second Response "Lead fills out your form at 11:47pm Tuesday. This is Alex. Watch." SMS sending in seconds
2. Qualification "Jordan asks 4 questions. Never diagnoses. Never quotes prices. Only books." SMS conversation thread
3. Booking + CRM "Lead picks a time. Your phone gets a push. Morgan logged everything." Lead card in dashboard
4. Follow-Up "For leads that don't book immediately — Riley sends 4 messages over 7 days." Follow-up sequence view
5. Dispatch "Zip 77084, AC repair, Thursday 2pm — Sam assigns the right tech automatically." Dispatch assignment screen
After the demo: "That's the whole system. Five people, five jobs, 24/7. What questions do you have right now?" — Then stop talking. Let them react. Do not fill the silence.

Objection Handling
Hormozi Root Cause Diagnosis
Every Objection Is One of Three Problems
Before answering any objection, identify the root. Then solve at the root — not the symptom.
Root CauseWhat They're Really SayingFix It With
Outcome Belief "I don't think this will actually work" Proof + the 30-day free trial eliminates their risk
Trust in You "I don't know if YOU can deliver it" Specificity, transparency, case studies, your personal involvement
Perceived Cost "The money/time/disruption isn't worth it" ROI math — one extra job vs. months of cost
💭
"How do I know this will actually work?"
Outcome Belief
"That's exactly why I'm giving you 30 days for free. You're not buying a promise — you're running it on your actual leads. By day 7 you'll have seen it work or you won't. You risk nothing except 30 days of having your leads followed up on instantly. Leads contacted within 5 minutes convert at 100x the rate of leads contacted after an hour. That's not my stat — that's industry research. The system does that for every single lead."
💭
"I already have someone doing follow-up"
Outcome Belief
"Does she work at 2am? Does she send a follow-up at exactly 1 hour, 24 hours, 72 hours, and 7 days? Does she qualify every lead with the same 4 questions and log it in a CRM automatically? I'm not replacing her — I'm handling the overflow and after-hours so she can focus on the jobs that actually need a human. What would it mean for her if every lead that came in after 5pm was already qualified and booked by the time she got in?"
💭
"I don't trust AI talking to my customers"
Trust + Fear
"I built the system with this exact concern from HVAC owners in mind. The AI does one thing: it collects four pieces of information and books a time slot. It cannot diagnose. It cannot quote. It cannot make any technical statement about the problem. If someone asks 'what's wrong with my AC?' — it says 'great question, let's get a tech out to take a look.' You see every single message in the dashboard. You can step in at any point. And every customer who does book — they don't feel like they're talking to a robot. They feel like they got a quick, professional response."
💭
"$297 is a lot every month"
Perceived Cost
"What's your average job ticket? [They answer.] So one extra job a month that would have gone cold pays for 20 months of the system. You're doing ~40 leads a month — if this rescues even 2 of those, that's [2× ticket]. The $297 is literally rounding error. The real question isn't whether $297 is worth it. It's: are you losing more than $297 worth of jobs to slow follow-up right now? I think we both know the answer."
💭
"I need to think about it"
Trust (usually)
"Totally fair. What's the part that needs more thought? Is it the tech side, is it whether this will work for HVAC specifically, or is it something else?" [Let them answer — it's one of the other objections in disguise. Solve that. Then:] "The 30 days free eliminates the financial risk entirely. Worst case: you spend 30 days having your leads followed up on automatically and it doesn't add any jobs. Then you walk away and owe nothing. Does it make more sense to think about it — or to just try it and see?"
💭
"Can you send me more information?"
Trust / Stall
"Absolutely — I'll send you a one-pager after the call. But let me ask: if the information confirmed everything I've just told you, would you be ready to try the 30-day pilot?" [If yes:] "Then let's save you a step. What would it take for you to be comfortable moving forward today?" [If no:] "What's the question that the information needs to answer?"
💭
"I need to talk to my partner/wife first"
Stakeholder
"Makes sense — would it be helpful to do a quick 10-minute call with both of you so she can ask questions directly? I can do that this week." [Then:] "In the meantime — can I ask what YOU think? If it was just your call, does this make sense?" Get the champion committed first. Then loop in the other person.
