## SalesRabbit

**Best at:** door-to-door for home services (HVAC, solar, roofing, pest control, lawn care, alarms). The most-mentioned platform in D2D communities and the de-facto choice for residential field sales.

**Strengths:**
- Lead tracking with disposition codes designed for D2D (knock results, follow-up flags)
- Native canvassing app with offline mode
- Built-in proposal and contract tools for some verticals
- Gamification + leaderboards
- Marketplace for lead data and add-ons

**Weaknesses:**
- Less strong on B2B SMB (restaurant / gas station) than on residential
- Pricing isn't fully transparent — call for quote past the base tier
- Integration with general-purpose CRMs (HubSpot, Salesforce) is workable but not the polish point

**Team-size fit:** 2-50 reps. Sweet spot is 5-20.

**Vertical fit:** home services, alarms, solar, roofing, pest control.

**Rough pricing:** entry tier ~$25-35/user/mo, full feature set $75-150/user/mo. Verify.

## Spotio

**Best at:** territory mapping + multichannel field-sales engagement for B2B field reps.

**Strengths:**
- Strong territory definition + assignment (draw polygons, lock reps to areas)
- Multichannel — combines in-person visits with email and SMS follow-up tracking
- CRM integrations are first-class (Salesforce, HubSpot, Microsoft Dynamics)
- Activity tracking that survives an offline shift

**Weaknesses:**
- More expensive than D2D-only tools
- Heavier setup — territory definition takes upfront work
- Overkill for a solo founder or 2-rep team

**Team-size fit:** 5-50 reps.

**Vertical fit:** B2B SMB (restaurants, gas stations, salons), distribution reps, multi-state expansion.

**Rough pricing:** $50-130/user/mo depending on tier. Verify.

## Map My Customers

**Best at:** mobile-first CRM for outside sales with simpler route planning than Spotio.

**Strengths:**
- Mobile app polish — the "do everything from the truck" experience
- Native route planning + drive-time optimization
- Lighter setup than Spotio
- Good fit for reps who'd previously been working from a spreadsheet

**Weaknesses:**
- Less depth on territory definition / assignment
- Reporting is basic compared to Spotio
- Fewer multichannel features

**Team-size fit:** 1-25 reps.

**Vertical fit:** distribution, wholesale reps, contractors, B2B SMB outside sales.

**Rough pricing:** $40-80/user/mo. Verify.

## Badger Maps

**Best at:** route optimization for outside reps. Pure routing — not a CRM.

**Strengths:**
- Best-in-class route optimization (drive-time clustering, lasso selection, daily route export)
- Quick onboarding — under an hour from sign-up to running a route
- Integrates with Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive — sits alongside an existing CRM
- Solo-founder friendly

**Weaknesses:**
- Not a CRM — you need one alongside it
- No canvassing-specific features (door dispositions, knock tracking)
- Pure routing means it can't replace SalesRabbit/Spotio for a full D2D motion

**Team-size fit:** 1-50 reps.

**Vertical fit:** any outside-sales motion where routing is the bottleneck.

**Rough pricing:** ~$58/user/mo standard, ~$95/user/mo enterprise. Verify.

## Outfield

**Best at:** canvassing tracking with team gamification.

**Strengths:**
- Visit logging with photo + GPS proof
- Team leaderboards and gamification
- Form templates for repeat visit types (audit, recap, follow-up)
- Modest pricing

**Weaknesses:**
- Smaller user base than SalesRabbit / Spotio — community help is thin
- CRM features are basic; treat it as activity tracking, not the system of record
- Vertical depth varies — strongest in retail / merchandising contexts

**Team-size fit:** 2-30 reps.

**Vertical fit:** retail merchandising, beverage / FMCG reps, any canvassing motion needing photo proof.

**Rough pricing:** $39-79/user/mo. Verify.

## Repsly

**Best at:** retail execution — reps visit stores to merchandise, audit shelves, take orders.

**Strengths:**
- Built specifically for CPG / FMCG retail execution
- Strong on-shelf audit features (planogram compliance, photo proof, share-of-shelf reporting)
- Order capture in-shift
- Multi-country support including emerging markets

**Weaknesses:**
- Wrong tool for a pure sales-acquisition motion — designed for visiting existing accounts
- Heavier feature set than a startup needs

**Team-size fit:** 10-200 reps. Enterprise-leaning.

**Vertical fit:** CPG, beverage, snack, retail merchandising.

**Rough pricing:** quote-based, typically $50-100/user/mo. Verify.

---

## Skynamo

**Best at:** FMCG / distribution field sales with order capture in-shift.

**Strengths:**
- Order capture is first-class — reps quote and book in the field
- Strong territory and route planning
- Built for distribution / wholesale verticals
- Integrates with accounting (Sage, Xero, QuickBooks)

**Weaknesses:**
- Lighter on consumer-vertical D2D
- Pricing isn't transparent

**Team-size fit:** 5-100 reps.

**Vertical fit:** FMCG distribution, wholesale, agriculture, building materials.

**Rough pricing:** quote-based. Verify.

---

## Geopointe

**Best at:** Salesforce-native geolocation, mapping, and route planning. Lives inside Salesforce.

**Strengths:**
- Pure-Salesforce native — no separate platform to log into
- Plots any Salesforce object on a map (accounts, opportunities, custom)
- Route optimization driven from Salesforce data
- Strong for orgs already standardized on Salesforce

**Weaknesses:**
- Locked to Salesforce — useless without it
- Less polish than purpose-built field apps for D2D-specific workflows
- Doesn't fix Salesforce's mobile UX shortcomings on its own

**Team-size fit:** 5-500 reps. Enterprise Salesforce orgs.

**Vertical fit:** any vertical where the org is already Salesforce-standardized.

**Rough pricing:** ~$55/user/mo Standard. Verify.

| Pure routing optimization next to existing CRM | Badger Maps |

| FMCG distribution with order capture | Skynamo |
| Salesforce-standardized org | Geopointe |
